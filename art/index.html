Large-scale projects built in the desert. Teams of volunteers, multi-year timelines, and structures that were always meant to burn.

Temple of Transition

2011

A 120-foot tall structure of six outer temples surrounding a central tower, connected by intricate wooden bridges. Inspired by ancient cathedrals and Hindu temples. For two weeks, it held the world record for the largest temporary wooden structure.

Participants left messages, mementos, and offerings symbolizing grief and loss. On the final night, the temple burned in a ceremony of collective catharsis. The work was featured in Business Insider, Rolling Stone, CNN, Vice, and Smithsonian Magazine.

  • Lead on a team of 200+ volunteers across 8 months
  • Design, carpentry, metalwork, pyrotechnics, lighting
  • Seven months modular construction, one month desert build
  • Buddhist and Eastern philosophical influences throughout

La Llorona

2015

A weeping woman rising from the playa. Mixed-media sculpture combining wood, metal, and LED lighting systems. My last large installation before stepping back from the festival.

  • Electrical systems for 500+ LED nodes
