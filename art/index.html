Art Portfolio

This portfolio includes photos of my notable work as a lead on teams of 20–200, helping to dream up and create large-scale immersive art installations for the Burning Man Project. These were some of the most ambitious installations in the event's history, and have been featured in major publications such as Business Insider, Rolling Stone, CNN, Vice, and Smithsonian Magazine.

My artistic contributions included all mediums necessary to actualize these projects: design, carpentry, metalwork, pyrotechnics, lighting, and more—from conception to completion. Project builds typically consisted of 8 months of full-time work: 7 months constructing the modular components in town, and one month building onsite in the harsh conditions of the Black Rock Desert.

Through my art, I strive to create environments to immerse the audience in dreams-made-real, evoking a childlike sense of wonder and inviting reflection on themes of joy, grief, and the power of relationships. I believe that art has the power to transform and uplift, to challenge and inspire, and to connect us all in ways that transcend language and culture. My primary artistic influences are Buddhism and Eastern philosophy more broadly, as well as traditional art and architecture from around the world.

The Temple of Transition, 2011

International Arts Megacrew. The Temple of Transition was 120-ft tall structure consisting of six outer temples surrounding a central temple, connected by intricate wooden bridges, with a design inspired by ancient cathedrals and Hindu temples. Participants left messages, mementos, and offerings symbolizing grief and loss. On the final night, the temple was solemnly and silently burned in a ceremony of collective catharsis. For two weeks, the temple held the world record for the largest temporary wooden structure.
