About

I spent years building large-scale physical systems: temple installations at Burning Man, off-grid infrastructure, touring logistics. Coordinating 60-person crews, managing complex timelines, designing systems that had to work under extreme constraints. The work was about emergent order: getting complex groups to accomplish hard things.

I pivoted to software because bits scale better than atoms. The fundamental interest stayed the same: systems architecture, high-leverage coordination, tools that amplify capability. I'm drawn to problems where careful design creates order from complexity.

B.S. EECS, UC Berkeley, graduating May 2026. Regents' and Chancellor's Scholarship recipient. Recent work: co-authored ML research paper on kernel ridge regression (arXiv:2410.02680), lead developer on Berkeley-funded RAG platform for course-specific AI tutors (FastAPI/Docker/Pinecone stack), and automation tools for knowledge work.
