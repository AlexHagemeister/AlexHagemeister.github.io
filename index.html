About

B.S. EECS, UC Berkeley, graduating May 2026. Regents' and Chancellor's Scholarship recipient.

AI capability is accelerating faster than most can fully grasp, but most organizations are still figuring out how to use it for anything beyond chatbots, demo days, or straight up vaporware. What AI can technically do will continue to outpace what companies and developers can actually get out of them. The race is here.

That gap is my target for the next 2-5 years: the infrastructure that takes raw AI capability and makes it work inside real workflows with messy data and vague requirements. The connective tissue between a model and a problem that gets solved. RAG pipelines, document processing, intelligent automation, and the like.

Writing code is getting commoditized. What isn't: figuring out what needs to exist, designing the system that gets you there, and making it run reliably in production where nobody's solved this exact problem before. That's what I'm after. Scoping ambiguous problems, building infrastructure, shipping systems that work at the point of use.

I'm targeting early-stage teams building AI platforms where the work matters: climate tech, biotech, productivity tools, applied research. More than the vertical, I want small teams, high trust, ill-defined problems, and real autonomy from problem definition through deployment. The kind of place where "figure it out" is the job description.

Selected Work

Highly Adaptive Ridge

Co-authored ML research paper as sole undergraduate on Real World Methods, a graduate research team within UC Berkeley's Center for Targeted Machine Learning (led by Professors Schuler & van der Laan). Developed kernel ridge regression method achieving n-1/3 dimension-free L2 convergence rate, empirically outperforming state-of-the-art on small tabular datasets. arXiv:2410.02680

SPLAT – AI Course Tutors, UC Berkeley

Lead developer on Berkeley-funded EdTech platform for course-specific AI tutors. Designed and built the full stack: automated pipeline to transform heterogeneous course materials (PDFs, LaTeX, slides) into chunked vector embeddings, semantic retrieval via Pinecone, context-aware response generation through OpenAI APIs, and an interactive student-facing frontend. FastAPI, Docker, Firebase, LangChain. Deployed for multiple Berkeley courses.

IT Service Desk Intern – Stanford Research Institute

Embedded technical support for SRI researchers. The job was nominally IT — VPN issues, network access, system diagnostics — but the actual work was going into labs and offices where researchers were stuck on problems outside their expertise and getting them unblocked. Processed 300+ service requests in three months. Learned firsthand how much real research depends on reliable infrastructure, and what it feels like to be the person who makes the work possible when the tools fail.
